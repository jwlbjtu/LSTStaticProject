Let's Shine Together

Love a Child

Children of the incarcerated are a wounded and forgotten group living in shadows.
Let's Shine Together exists to bring companionship, warmth, and hope to let them embrace the society.

Children are Innocent

Children of the incarcerated are victims of their parent's incrimination. They are traumatized by the arrest and loss of their parents, and sometimes even the criminal acts of their parents. The incarceration of their parents deprived the parental love every child should enjoy, and subjects these children to financial hardship, psychological trauma, and discrimination from the society.

All these unintended punishment on these innocent children could destroy their self-esteem, their education prospect, and their sense of belonging in the society. They are cast in the shadow and need people's love and attention.

OUR STORY

Our story begins with a little girl named Wang Ruizhi in Dongzhou Children's Village, Shaanxi, China. Her parents were incarcerated and she was sent to the Children's Village to save her from straying on the street. In 2009, from a group of volunteers, Ruizhi held Karen's hand as a way to say she selected Karen to be her "Big Sister" to give her mentorship in the coming 4 months. Since then, Karen visited her in the Village, wrote letters full of pictures to her, and mailed clothings and school supplies to her.

After six months, Karen moved to the United States and lost contact with Ruizhi, but Karen never forgot about Ruizhi and the incredible and lovely children in Dongzhou Children's Village. In 2015, Karen came up with "Let's Shine Together" Project to organize summer and winter camps, and her friend Ellie immediately jumped in to help. Together, they were able to gather a magical team of 20 to head to Dongzhou Children's Village to celebrate Christmas and New Year with the children in a fun winter camp.

A filming team of 4 also will film a documentary so more people can know the life of a child with incarcerated parents.

WHAT WE DO

We bring love to children of the incarcerated.

We hold summer and winter camps to bring love, warmth, and company to these children in need.

We raise funds to provide living support and school tuitions for these children to keep them in school.

We hold psychiatrist sessions to help children overcome their trauma.

We make "Bed Time Story Box" so that these children can listen to stories before they go to bed every day.
