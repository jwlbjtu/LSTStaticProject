Love a Child

Children of the incarcerated are a wounded and forgotten group living in shadows.
Let's Shine Together exists to bring companionship, warmth, and hope to let these children embrace the society.

Children are Innocent

Children of the incarcerated are victims of their parent's incrimination. They are traumatized by the arrest and loss of their parents, and sometimes even the criminal acts of their parents. The incarceration of their parents deprived them the parental love every child should enjoy, and subjects these children to financial hardship, psychological trauma, and discrimination from the society.

All these unintended punishment on these innocent children could destroy their self-esteem, their education prospect, and their sense of belonging in the society. They are cast in the shadow and need people's love and attention.

OUR STORY

Our story began with a little girl named Wang Ruizhi in Dongzhou Children's Village, Shaanxi, China. Her parents were incarcerated and she was sent to the Children's Village to save her from straying on the street. In 2009, from a group of volunteers, Ruizhi held Karen's hand as a way to say she selected Karen to be her "Big Sister" to give her mentorship in the coming 4 months. Since then, Karen visited her in the Village, wrote letters full of pictures to her, and mailed clothings and school supplies to her.

After six months, Karen moved to the United States and lost contact with Ruizhi, but Karen never forgot about Ruizhi and the incredible and lovely children in Dongzhou Children's Village. In 2015, Karen came up with "Let's Shine Together" Project to organize summer and winter camps, and her friend Ellie immediately jumped in to help.
